# Thinking Prompts for Building the Presentation

Use these prompts to dialogue with the material and find your own story.

## Thesis

- What is the simplest sentence that captures what has changed?
- Is the talk about AI, software economics, marketing operations, or human agency?
- What do you believe that the audience may not yet believe?

## Audience

- What does a marketer need to do differently on Monday morning?
- What does a leader need to stop doing?
- What fears in the room are legitimate, and which are excuses?

## Narrative flow

- Do you want to start with the MarTech explosion, or with a human story of frustration/building?
- Which case study is the emotional center: Andrea, DishList, Theo/Frame.io, or your Polly interview?
- Where should the talk pivot from possibility to responsibility/security?

## Build vs buy

- What is still worth buying?
- What is now worth building?
- What criteria matter now: uniqueness, trust, compliance, data moats, verification cost, integration burden?

## Role shift

- If developers become orchestrators, what do marketers become?
- Is "requirements are expensive" really another way of saying "taste and judgment are expensive"?
- How do context and memory become infrastructure rather than prompt tricks?

## Ending

- What is the call to action: build one internal tool, audit your SaaS stack, learn to specify requirements, create a team skill library, adopt agents safely?
- What final line makes the audience feel urgency without hype?
